Sierra Leone

Sierra Leone is a country of about 5 million. The capital city is Freetown, where about 470,000 people live. The name Sierra Leone comes from "lion mountain" from the mountainous peninsula where Freetown is. Sierra Leone's largest ethnic groups are the Temne and the Mende, along with a smaller population of Krios, who are descendants of slaves. Sierra Leone achieved independence in 1961. A one-party system controlled the government for two decades, which was overthrown following a Liberian-aided rebel group, the Revolutionary United Front (RUF). In 1992 another group of army officers staged a coup, which incited a civil war that killed around 15,000 people and drove millions of people from their homes. The RUF recruited thousands of child soldiers. In 1996 Sierra Leoneans voted to elect Ahmen Tejan Kabbah for the Sierra Leone People's Party (SLPP). In 1997 the same RUF army officers staged another coup leading Foday Sankoh to power until the Economic Community of West African States brought its peacekeeping force to restore Kabbah as president. The UN helped sponsored a peace deal in 1999; Kabbah continued as president, and Sankoh the power to lead the diamond mining. In April 2000, The RUF captured hundreds of UN soldiers and headed towards Freetown; this caused international alarm, and the UK sent troops to arrest Sankoh. By 2001 the UN demobilized most of the RUF soldiers. Kabbah was returned to the presidency in 2002. In 2007 Ernest Bai Koroma of the All People's Congress (APC) was elected. 2010 brought the lifting of sanctions by the UN, and Charles Taylor, the former Liberian leader, was convicted of crimes against humanity that he committed in Sierra Leone civil war. In 2014 Sierra Leone battled the Ebola epidemic; a state of emergency was declared, and movement restrictions enforced. Sierra Leone was declared free of ebola in November 2015 after 3,500 people died.
