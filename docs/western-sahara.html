Western Sahara

Western Sahara is on the north-west coast of Africa. Almost entirely desert, this territory is occupied by Moroccan troops. Its capital city is L'ayoune. Western Sahara is home to the Saharawi people, about 90,000 of whom live in refugee camps in Algeria. The country has been flooded with 200,000 Moroccan settlers. More than 100,000 Moroccan soldiers also occupy it. Western Sahara was a Spanish colony. In 1973, Polisario's armed liberation movement was formed, but when Spain gave up the territory, they gave the northern two-thirds to Morocco and the southern third to Mauritania. The International Court of Justice ruled Western Sahara was entitled to independence, but Morocco refused. Mauritania withdrew after years of fighting with Polisario, and Morocco seized their third of the country. In the 1980s, more than 60 countries recognized Western Sahara as a legitimate government, and in 1990, the UN-brokered a peace plan that led to a ceasefire declared in 1991. Since then, there has been very little progress on a referendum as Morocco refuses to hold the referendum.
