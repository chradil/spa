description
Guinea-Bissau is a West-African country with a population of 1.5 million. 30% of this population lives in urban areas, about 400,000 of whom live in Bissau the capital city. Guinea-Bissau has a large number of ethnic groups, the Balanta and Fulani accounting for about half of the population. Two-thirds of the population live below the poverty line and life expectancy is 48 years and it one of the lowest in the world. Education standards are poor and only about half of the children are enrolled. About 60% of the country's GDP comes from agricutlure; crops such as rice, millet, sorghum, cassava and other root crops are exported. The main cash crop is cashew nuts. Guinea-Bissea became independent in 1974 and was federated with Cape Verde. Between 1974 and 1999 the country was ruled by the Partido Africano da Independencia de Guine e Cabo Verde (PAIGC). In 1980 the party was seized by General Joao Bernardo Vieira in a coup; he broke Guinea-Bisseau from Cape Verde, opening the goverment to other political parites in the 1990s. Vieira retained control until 1999 when he was overthrown and presidental and legislative electiosn were won, established Kumba Yala as president. Yala died in 2014 and his presidentcy is remembered as "erratic and incompetenet". In 2003 the miltary intervened and in 2005 Viera was voted back into office - he was then assassinated in 2009. Malam Bacai Sanha won the presidential eleciton in 2012 and died later that year. He was replaced by Carlost Gomes Junior and another military coups ensued driving Gomes to exile in Portugal.
