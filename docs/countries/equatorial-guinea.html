Equatorial Guinea

Equatorial Guinea is a country with a coastal plain, forested hills, plateau, and a few islands, the largest being Bioko, 200 km north of the Gulf of Guinea. The island Bioko is also home to the capital city of Malabo, which has a population of 166,000. Originally colonized by the Spanish, Equatorial Guinea thrived off of the cocoa plantations before independence. In 1991 oil was discovered off the coast of Bioko, and by 2009 it amounted to 90% of the country's GDP. Despite independence, Equatorial Guinea has been under Spain's control. In 1979 Spain supported a violent coup putting Teodoro Obiang Nguema Mbasogo in power over his uncle, Francisco Macias Nguema. Both ruled as dictators. Obiang established a multiparty democracy in 1991 but won re-election in 1996, 2002, 2009 using fraud and intimation to do so.
