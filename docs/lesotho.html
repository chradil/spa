description
Eswatini (formerly Swaziland) is a landlocked country on the eastern side of South Africa. It is about 110 miles long from north to south and 80 miles wide. Colonized by the British, the colonial administration held its capital in Mbabane, which remains its administrative center. In 2018 the king announced a name change of the country from the Kingdom of Swaziland to the Kingdom of Eswatini. Eswatini is governed by a king who appoints a prime minister and a cabinet and a House of Assembly who are elected by popular vote. Land ownership remains one of the most challenging issues in the country, in large part due to the shift in power after colonial rule between Britain and the king. Education was established formerly by missionaries in pre-colonial times, and in 1906 the Swazi nation set up schools known as "tribal" schools. After independence, primary and secondary schools were set up, and more than 80% of school-age children attend.
